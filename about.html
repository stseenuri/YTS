The Real Story

How I Went From Student to Teacher

Let me be honest with you.

I didn't grow up wanting to be a yoga teacher. I wasn't the spiritual kid who meditated at 5 AM. I was just a regular person dealing with my own health problems‚Äîthe kind doctors couldn't really fix with pills.

That's when I found yoga. Actually, yoga found me.

And I fell in love with it. Not because it looked cool on Instagram. But because after years of trying everything else‚Äîmedicines, diets, treatments‚Äîthis actually worked. My body felt better. My mind felt calmer. The problems I thought were "just how I am" started going away.

5 Years of Practice Before Teaching

I practiced for 5 whole years before teaching a single student.

Most people finish their yoga teacher training and immediately start teaching. I didn't. I wanted to really understand how the body works. Why certain poses help with certain conditions. What makes therapeutic yoga different from regular exercise.

I got my 200-Hour Yoga Teacher Training certification. Then I specialized in therapeutic yoga for health conditions. Then I kept practicing, kept learning, kept reading.

Because here's what I realized: Teaching yoga isn't just about showing poses. It's about understanding what's happening inside someone's body when they're in pain, when they can't sleep, when their hormones are all over the place, when they're stressed out of their mind.

That's the yoga I teach. Not the fancy stuff. The healing stuff.

Teaching Philosophy

What You Can Expect From My Classes

I teach differently than most yoga instructors. Here's how:

01

No Fancy Instagram Poses

I don't care if you can do a headstand. I care if your back pain goes away. We focus on therapeutic poses that actually fix your specific problem‚Äînot what looks good in photos.

02

Your Pace, Not Mine

Yoga isn't a competition. If you need to rest, rest. If a pose hurts, we modify it. I'll never push you into something your body isn't ready for. Progress happens slowly‚Äîand that's perfectly fine.

03

Real Corrections, Every Class

Small groups mean I can actually see your screen, watch your posture, and tell you exactly what to adjust. You're not just another face in a 100-person Zoom room. I remember your name and your concerns.

04

Speak Your Language

If you're more comfortable in Telugu or Kannada, we'll do the class in that. Because when you're holding a difficult pose, you shouldn't have to translate instructions in your head.

05

Science + Practice

I'll explain WHY we're doing a pose. What it does to your organs, your hormones, your nervous system. You're not just following along blindly‚Äîyou're understanding how your body heals.

06

Patience Above Everything

Some students see results in a week. Some take two months. Everyone's body is different. I won't rush you or make you feel bad for going slow. We're here for healing, not speed records.
